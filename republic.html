Roman Republic

There was one main issue with the early roman empire and that was that majority of the people who created majority of the rules were patricians. This meant that the minority had the most amount of power and the majority had the least amount of power, this was a very unbalanced and unrepresentative system.

After a variety of outrages from the plebeians, (the majority of the roman empire), From 471 BCE and later, new laws were introduced to create a more fair system. To start of, Tribunes, who were voted by plebeians were introduced. Tribunes could take part of the 600 people inside the senate. When someone joins the senate, they cannot leave the senate without permission of the senator. ‘with no senator being able to leave Italy without the Senate's approval’ (Mark Cartwright, Published December, 2016). Senators were also not paid anything.
