Roman War

The roman army was structured differently than other armies. It was split into two main sections, people who were citizens and people who weren’t. These are also called Legionaries (Citizens) and Auxiliaries (Non-Citizens). 8 Legionaries create a Contubernium which share sleeping quarters. In the Contubernium, Newer soldiers who were less experienced usually got the more boring and dirty jobs such as cleaning, grinding rocks, etc. 10 contubernium makes a century, each century has commanders such as the tesserarius and centurion. 6 centuries make a cohort, 10 cohorts make a Legion. The first Cohort (Cohort I) is larger, where being doubled. The legatus is the commander of the entire legion. Auxiliaries are more specialists, using their skills and spreading out. Auxiliaries don’t have Legions, just Cohorts. After 25 years of service, Auxiliaries get their own land and citizenship rights.
