Roman Technology

Rome was known for being revolutionary and ‘ahead of its time’ when it existed. Some of these technologies are:

Roman Numerals

An image of Italy

Alex1011 [CC BY-SA 3.0], via Wikimedia Commons

Number Systems before Roman Numerals were very primitive. Older Number systems used cutting notches on walls, and each notch would be 1. This method takes a long time when dealing with larger numbers and is difficult to read large numbers. This is where the Roman system improves on that. Roman Numerals use a base 10 system, like the decimal system but they didn’t use place value. Romans used Symbols such as I, V, X, L, C… etc.

Aqueducts

The purpose of aqueducts were to transport water from one side to another while also being able to act as a bridge. The first roman aqueducts were built in 312 BCE. The first aqueduct was located in Roma, Italy. (© 2004 - 2019 Cees W. Passchier, Last updated January 2019). Aqueducts commonly had an design that included lots of arches.

Why I ranked this 1st:

Roman Technology has been ranked 1st as it has made the most difference in today's society. Inventions such as Roman Numerals, Newspapers, Julian Calendars, Aqueducts and more have made a difference. Roman Numerals has evolved into the Decimal System, the Julian Calendar has evolved to the Gregorian Calendar and Newspapers were possible because of Rome’s first, early solution. Roman Technology has a higher level of importance than Roman Republic because Roman Technology has impacted the present society much more. Some of the technologies affected were Newspapers, Number systems, Aqueducts, Plumbing, etc.
