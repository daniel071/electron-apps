Spartacus Interview

Please note: These were not actual quotes from Spartacus, these are just interpretations of Spartacus’ opinions based on sources.

Interviewer: Did you choose or were forced to become a Gladiator?

Spartacus: I was originally a slave, forced into becoming a gladiator. I’ve had enough of fighting for others, making the promise to be burned, bound, beaten so 76 other slaves and I fought out way out. We used weapons we found in the kitchen and ran for it.

Interviewer: Did you enjoy being a gladiator?

Spartacus: No I did not enjoy being a gladiator. I’ve had enough of being disciplined and ted it to change. While fighting for freedom was difficult, I did enjoy fighting for freedom with others with the same goal.

Interviewer: Were you in the Roman Army and if you were, what did you do there?

Spartacus: I was not in the Roman Army, the Roman Army was against me when I made the escape but centurions finished me off.

Interviewer: Did other people consider you skilled on your job?

Spartacus: I was very well known since I was the first one who started one of many of escapes. Many other people decided to do the same thing and I was their inspiration. One issue was that majority of these escapes I inspired were disruptive to rome. Nowadays, my name is associated with ‘being unique’ as well as ‘freedom’. Greek Historian Plutarch has once stated, 'Spartacus . . . was a Thracian from the nomadic tribes and not only had a great spirit and great physical strength, but was . . . most intelligent and cultured' (Jacaranda, Published 2017). When Plutarch claimed I was 'intelligent', he most likely meant that I was strategic and made good decisions. This is an accurate and reliable source because it came from an Greek Historian who lived during the same time as I.
